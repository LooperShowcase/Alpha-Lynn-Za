Character Development

Experience

Character Development, ESPECIALLY if your making a character in a game or TV show, is extremely important for both viewers to see how characters have changed, and for the experiences your character has gone through. A few examples are: South Park, God of War, Adventure time, etc... Many of these characters are developed by experiences, episodes, the continuation of the game or something else! Your character is most likely to have an entire change due to development. Whether it be from trauma, learning, growing/maturing or others!

Types of Character Development and experiences

Of course, not all experiences and developments are the same! We'll be talking about different types of experiences here.

Traumatic

A traumatic experience is likely to alter your character's personality, and or view of the world, and it will most likely have a forever-lasting effect on them. Causing development. Character trauma, similar to actual trauma, can be based off of anything! Like your character slipping on a puddle and embarrassing themselves, being nearly ran over by a car, or accidentally almost drowning!

Maturity

Maturing is something often depicted in characters in cartoons or art. Many characters 'grow up' by having a simple change of voice or appearance. Growing taller, bigger, etc... Though sometimes Maturity is often shown in personality instead. Maturity can also be shown through puberty.

Learning

A character can develop from a new journey or experience/lesson. Even from your character's daily life, they can still learn and develop! Just like people, characters also have their own life and lifestyle. Everyday, like people, they go outside of their home on an adventure, to work, to school, etc... And learn something new from a first-time experience of something.

Some characters can face development in different ways, even other than this! Though we won't be exploring that today, as this first website would be WAYYYYYYY too long.
