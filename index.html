A Guide To Character Creation and Development

Character Creation

Character creation is an extremely complicated and difficult process. Whether it's what clothes your characters wear, hairstyle, facial features or the height of your character! This is a guide to aide you on your journey to character creation!

Shape

Character shapes are extremely important to recognizing your favorite character, similar to how it's possible to recognize all of your favorite cartoon characters from your favorite television shows. Shape is an important asset to making your character recognizable within every image.

Simplicity

Simplicty is another important asset to your character, and can make the visual appearance of a character more likeable to viewers. Especially within art, games, or TV shows! Don't overcomplicate every feature, remove an outline or two or make your character have a specific expression they like to show! Though it's important to not oversimplify a character, as it can cause the design to be plain and boring to viewers.

Facial features

The facial features of a character are essential to creation. A character's face can reveal an entire personality to viewers about your character! Soft or more feminine features can reveal shyness, while more sharp features can reveal a serious persona within your character! Especially in games or art.

Body Type

Height, structure and figure. A character's body is always important to development and personality! Some characters may have strong builds, with large muscles and tall figures. While other characters may be skinny, short, chubby, etc... Character's bodies can also be linked with a past, which gives viewers either an idea of what your character has gone through, or what they will develop into in the future of the show, art, or game your creating!

Artstyles

Each character has a better appearance in different artstyles, and it's extremely important to experiment with your character in different styles to eventually create one that matches the character perfectly. For example, in a popular cartoon show "Steven Universe", every character is different with many shapes, facial features and different body types. Though not too different that you would be unable to conclude that those two characters are both from the same show. It's important to have different artstyles when drawing, though remember to always draw in the artstyle you prefer!

Poses and Perspective

When drawing a character, three of the most important things to firstly do is: 1. Draw them in different Poses! 2. Experiment with style and perspective! 3. Plan ahead!

Drawing a character in different poses can give you an idea of how tall the character is, how to draw features properly, and what they look like from different angles! A hairstyle or a shirt from the back can be extremely different from the front.

Emotions

Emotions are very complicated, especially in people. Though is it the same case for your imaginary characters? Well, the answer is obviously yes, though let's talk more into Why?

Emotions are especially difficult to place into characters due to needing experience. To make a character act this way, or change themselves because of something they've gone through is difficult due to you not actually experiencing what they have gone through or will go through. The easiest way to allow your character to express themselves, however, is doing research. How real humans and people have reacted to this, what they did to cope, etc...
